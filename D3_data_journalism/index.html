Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Plotting various healthcare risks in a dynamic chart, allows us to explore possible correlations between health and livong standards

The scatter plot allows us to observe how the various states in the US fall on the scale, using data from the Census Bureau. If we plot Poverty against Healthcare, there are some clear outliers such as Texas.

Scarier trends are revealed if we plot Age against Obesity and notice that Louisiana, Mississippi and Arkansas are not doing well stopping obesity at a younger age.

We also notice that the Household income seems to correlate negatively with the percentage of smokers, with again Mississippi, Alaska and West Virginia showing the highest percentage and the lowest incomes. Data is awesome!
